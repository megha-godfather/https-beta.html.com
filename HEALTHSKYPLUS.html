DIAGNOSIS

  • COVID-19
    A highly contagious respiratory illness caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. Common symptoms include fever, cough, and fatigue. The virus spreads primarily through respiratory droplets when infected people talk, cough, or sneeze. Prevention includes vaccination, masks, and social distancing. Global impact has led to significant changes in healthcare and daily life.
  • Diabetes
    A chronic metabolic disorder characterized by elevated blood glucose levels due to either insufficient insulin production (Type 1) or the body's ineffective use of insulin (Type 2). The condition affects millions globally and its prevalence continues to rise, particularly in developing nations.
  • Hypertension
    A condition in which the force of the blood against the artery walls is too high. Often referred to as high blood pressure, it can lead to serious health issues such as heart disease and stroke. Common symptoms may include headaches, shortness of breath, and nosebleeds. Regular monitoring and lifestyle changes are key to management.
  • Asthma
    A chronic condition that affects the airways in the lungs, leading to difficulty breathing. Symptoms can include wheezing, coughing, chest tightness, and shortness of breath. Asthma can be triggered by allergens, exercise, and respiratory infections. Management includes avoiding triggers and using inhalers.
  • Arthritis
    A complex family of musculoskeletal disorders consisting of more than 100 different conditions that affect people of all ages, races, and genders. The two most common forms are osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis, each with distinct causes and treatments.
  • Depression
    A mood disorder that causes a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest. Depression can affect how you feel, think, and handle daily activities. Symptoms may include changes in appetite, sleep disturbances, and feelings of worthlessness. Treatment often includes therapy and medication.
  • Anxiety
    A mental health disorder characterized by feelings of worry, anxiety, or fear that are strong enough to interfere with daily activities. Symptoms can include restlessness, fatigue, and difficulty concentrating. Treatment options include therapy, medication, and lifestyle changes.
  • Migraine
    A type of headache characterized by severe pain, often accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light and sound. Migraines can last from hours to days and can be debilitating. Management includes medication and lifestyle adjustments to avoid triggers.
  • Thyroid Disorders
    Conditions that affect the thyroid gland, leading to an imbalance of hormones. Symptoms can include weight changes, fatigue, and mood swings. Common disorders are hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism. Treatment varies depending on the specific disorder and can include medication or surgery.
  • Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)
    A common disorder affecting the large intestine, characterized by symptoms such as cramping, abdominal pain, bloating, gas, and diarrhea or constipation. The exact cause is unknown, but management includes dietary changes and stress management techniques.
  • Multiple Sclerosis
    A complex autoimmune disease that affects the central nervous system (brain and spinal cord) by damaging the protective covering of nerve fibers (myelin sheath). This damage disrupts communication between the brain and the rest of the body, leading to a wide range of physical and cognitive symptoms.
  • Parkinson's Disease
    A progressive nervous system disorder affecting movement, characterized by tremors, stiffness, and difficulty with walking, balance, and coordination. The condition develops when nerve cells in the brain that produce dopamine become impaired or die, leading to various motor and non-motor symptoms.
  • Lupus
    A chronic autoimmune disease where the body's immune system becomes hyperactive and attacks normal, healthy tissue, causing inflammation and damage to various body systems including joints, skin, kidneys, blood cells, brain, heart, and lungs.
  • Celiac Disease
    An autoimmune disorder where the ingestion of gluten leads to damage in the small intestine. When people with celiac disease eat gluten (a protein found in wheat, rye, and barley), their body mounts an immune response that attacks the small intestine, damaging the villi that promote nutrient absorption.
  • Crohn's Disease
    A type of inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) that causes inflammation of the digestive tract, which can lead to abdominal pain, severe diarrhea, fatigue, weight loss, and malnutrition.
  • Fibromyalgia
    A chronic condition characterized by widespread musculoskeletal pain accompanied by fatigue, sleep, memory, and mood issues.
  • Endometriosis
    A painful disorder where tissue similar to the tissue that normally lines the inside of the uterus grows outside the uterus, causing severe pain and fertility problems.
  • Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)
    A progressive inflammatory lung disease that causes obstructed airflow from the lungs, typically caused by long-term exposure to irritating gases or particulate matter.
  • Sleep Apnea
    A potentially serious sleep disorder where breathing repeatedly stops and starts during sleep, significantly impacting quality of life.
  • Psoriasis
    A chronic autoimmune condition causing rapid buildup of skin cells, resulting in scaling on the skin's surface. Inflammation and redness around the scales is fairly common, and the scales typically develop into thick, red patches that can crack and bleed.
  • Epilepsy
    A central nervous system disorder causing abnormal brain activity leading to seizures or periods of unusual behavior, sensations, and sometimes loss of awareness.
  • Chronic Fatigue Syndrome
    A complex disorder characterized by extreme fatigue that can't be explained by any underlying medical condition, with symptoms that may worsen with physical or mental activity.
  • Wilson's Disease
    A rare inherited disorder causing excessive copper accumulation in the body, particularly in the liver, brain, and other vital organs.
